## 2. Top 5 — Full Sections

### Crack 1 — Mahler–Cartier rigidity for the Berg–Meinardus equation

**The bridge, precisely.** Berg–Meinardus 1994/95: Collatz ⟺ the only solutions holomorphic in |z|<1 of h(z³) = h(z⁶) + (1/3z)Σ_{j=0}^{2} ω^j h(ω^j z²) are c₀ + c₁·z/(1−z). The briefing agent's normalization (coefficientwise-verified): after cancelling z↦z³ and recognizing the root-of-unity average as a Cartier/section operator, the equation is **exactly** h ∈ ker L, L := 1 − M₂ − z·M₂·Λ₃,₂ where M_k h(z)=h(z^k) and Λ₃,₂(Σaₙzⁿ)=Σa₃ₙ₊₂zⁿ. Coefficientwise this is aₙ = a_{T(n)}: **ker L = functions constant on Collatz-graph components**; dim = 1 + #components; Collatz ⟺ dim 2. Every exotic cycle/divergent orbit contributes an indicator series h_C. L is *one mixed equation in the crossed algebra C(z)⟨M₂, Λ₃⟩* — sitting precisely in the gap that modern Mahler rigidity (pure 2-Mahler ∩ pure 3-Mahler = rational; the function-field Cobham/×2×3 phenomenon) has never touched.

**Prior-art map (honest).** Two-sided exhaustive search: the complete citation list of Berg–Meinardus 1994 (20 papers) contains **zero** Mahler-theory papers; Adamczewski–Bell (https://arxiv.org/abs/1303.2019), Schäfke–Singer (https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.02616), ADHW (https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.09266), ADH (https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.01874) contain zero Collatz. The single near-miss: Bell–Lagarias 2014 (https://arxiv.org/abs/1408.6884) — the same Bell — extends BM via Skolem–Mahler–Lech but never invokes the rigidity/Galois toolkit. Successor lines (Opfer 2011, withdrawn — http://preprint.math.uni-hamburg.de/public/papers/hbam/hbam2011-09.pdf; de Weger's rebuttal — https://web.archive.org/web/2013/https://www.win.tue.nl/~bdeweger/downloads/opfer-comments%20v0.2.pdf; Neklyudov https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.11859; Béhani https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.03203; Efrem https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.06736) grep clean for "Mahler". Order-1 machinery: Dreyfus–Hardouin–Roques (https://arxiv.org/abs/1507.03361); algorithms: Roques (https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.12995). Automata shadow: Dhiman–Pandey (https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06066).

**The push.** (i) **[PROVABLE-TODAY]** Corollary, apparently unrecorded: *no exotic Collatz component C has h_C satisfying both a 2-Mahler and a 3-Mahler equation* (Adamczewski–Bell ⇒ rational; Fatou/SML ⇒ eventually periodic; Monks 2006 ⇒ contradiction). Strictly extends Monks 2006; the functional-equation avatar of ×2×3 disjointness. (ii) **[PROGRAM]** The prize: one-sided rigidity — *no exotic solution of L is 2-Mahler at all* — which kills every 2-automatic/2-regular divergence certificate at every state count, subsuming and infinitely extending our ≤7-state DFA exhaustion. The checked closure lemma (BM rearranges to an inhomogeneous order-1 2-Mahler equation for M₃h, with μ_{3^k}-twists closing into finite M₂-systems) makes this an ADHW-style σ-Galois computation *one theorem past the literature* — honest caveats: Λ₃,₂ is not a field endomorphism (no existing Galois framework contains it); ker L is infinite-dimensional if Collatz fails, so Picard–Vessiot finiteness fails globally; the μ_{3^k}-twist tower growth is the precise technical obstruction.

**First move.** (a) *Lemma 0* (2-page note, publishable, stakes the bridge): the normalization, ker L = component-constants, and the Corollary. (b) *32-core computation, same day*: quantitative exclusion against the 2^71 frontier — any 2-Mahler certificate with annihilator complexity (r,d) is determined by an initial segment B(r,d); every component meeting [1,2^71] is trivial, so the error series must be a 2-Mahler function vanishing to order >2^71; sparse linear-algebra search over (r,d) for annihilators on n ≤ 10^7, conjecture-then-prove v(r,d) ≤ poly(d)·2^{O(r)} (Newton-polygon valuation lemma — good Lean target). (c) Galois track in parallel via Dreyfus–Hardouin–Roques order-1 classification.

**Success =** Lemma 0 published + the effective theorem "any 2-Mahler divergence certificate has complexity with v(r,d) > 2^71" (auto-strengthening with verification records). Jackpot (15–30% per the brief over 1–2 years) = the full "no 2-Mahler certificate" rigidity theorem — the analytic Cobham theorem for Collatz.

### Crack 2 — Termination-order barrier ladder (WQO, derivational complexity, arctic no-go)

**The bridge, precisely.** Zantema 2005 (https://www.win.tue.nl/~hzantema/torpa.pdf, Thm 16): SN of a 7-rule SRS Z ⟺ Collatz. Termination certificates stratify along two calibrated axes: ordinal strength of the order (simplification orders → matrix → ordinal interpretations → size-change) and the derivational-complexity ceiling each forces (match-bounds/arctic: linear; natural matrix: exponential; MPO: prim. rec.; LPO: mult. rec.; KBO: Ackermann). A Collatz proof = a point in this landscape; structural features of Collatz dynamics = exclusion theorems.

**Prior-art map (honest).** Exactly one serious paper: Yolcu–Aaronson–Heule (https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.14697) — no natural matrix interpretation of any dimension removes any rule of Z (Berstel pumping on 8n+1 ↦ 9n+2); grepped full text: they never mention simplification orders, self-embedding, or Kruskal, and explicitly leave the arctic case and system T open; no follow-up has closed either. Zantema's own derivation bh1³b →⁺ bh1⁵b is self-embedding and he never remarks on it. The reverse-math-of-termination-methods toolkit exists un-applied: FPSY (https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.09036, SCT ⟺ WO(ω^ω^ω)), Steila–Yokoyama (https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.08622). Touzet (https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/BFb0028014) is the methodological template — and its warning. Ladder sources: GHW04 (https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-540-70590-1_14), ZWM14 (http://cl-informatik.uibk.ac.at/users/swinkler/research/papers/ZWM14.pdf). Unreliable adjacents flagged: https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.09719.

**The push.** The brutal honest twist first: all known ordinal-unprovability technology detects *fast-growing witnesses*; Collatz's stopping time is conjecturally ~log n, so a "Collatz needs ordinal α" theorem **cannot** come from derivation lengths — question (b)'s dream is structurally dead **[PROVABLE-TODAY as a meta-observation]**. What survives is a rigorous two-axis barrier map: **Rung 1** [one line, new in print]: Z is self-embedding ⇒ *all* simplification orders of every ordinal strength fail (Dershowitz 1982). **Rung 2** [unconditional, numerically verified]: dc_Z(ℓ) = Ω(ℓ^{log₂3}) via the cascade n = 2^k−1 ⇒ every linear-dc method (match-bounds, single-step arctic) fails. **Rung 3** = YAH. **Rung 4 [LEMMA, the new theorem]**: arctic (max-plus) no-go via ultimate arithmetic-periodicity of one-letter max-plus rational series (cyclicity theorem) replacing Berstel — pigeonhole slope inequalities 8λ ≥ 9λ′ across residue classes to contradiction. Unifying insight: every excluded class is ℕ- or (max,+)-*rational*; the conjectured meta-barrier ("no rational/automatic certificate family witnesses Collatz") merges with our DFA exhaustion and points any real proof at non-rational invariants (2-adic valuation, measure — where Tao 2019 actually lives).

**First move.** Prove the arctic no-go (2–4 weeks; reducible-matrix corner cases are the fiddly part). Package with Rungs 1–2 (Lean: finite derivation + Higman-embedding check in a weekend; the dc bound is elementary). 32-core in parallel: SAT-search natural+arctic interpretations for the mixed-base system T at dimensions 1–6 — YAH's pumping provably does *not* extend to T, so T is where the next theorem or surprise lives.

**Success =** JAR/FSCD-level "termination lower-bound ladder for Collatz" closing YAH's open problem (≈70%), plus the regularity-barrier statement. Odds any of it proves Collatz: <2% — the value is retiring certificate classes before we waste compute searching them.

### Crack 3 — Exponential sums at the cycle modulus (Lee–Yang framing)

**The bridge, precisely.** Böhm–Sontacchi: cycles of signature (K,L) ⟺ admissible parity words v with M | W(v), W(v) = Σ 3^{L−1−i} 2^{b_i}, M = 2^K − 3^L. Hence #cycles(K,L) = (1/M) Σ_{ξ mod M} S_{K,L}(ξ), with S_{K,L}(ξ) = Σ_v e^{2πiξW(v)/M} a partition function with unit-circle (literal Lee–Yang locus) complex weights, computable by an inhomogeneous transfer matrix (state = #odd steps so far, O(K·L) per ξ). Word ↔ spin configuration; W ↔ energy; divergent orbits ↔ the ballistic subshift (prefix ones-density ≥ log2/log3), where Beraha–Kahane–Weiss equimodularity governs what finite transfer-matrix certificates can express.

**Prior-art map (honest).** The LD side is *mined*: Lagarias–Weiss (https://projecteuclid.org/journals/annals-of-applied-probability/volume-2/issue-1/The-3x--1-Problem-Two-Stochastic-Models/10.1214/aoap/1177005779.full), Kontorovich–Sinai/Kontorovich–Lagarias (https://arxiv.org/abs/0910.1944), Matthews–Watts (http://www.numbertheory.org/3x+1/), Krasikov–Lagarias (https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0205002 — the rigorous Perron/LP success story, x^0.84). The zero-locus/character-sum side is *virgin*: arXiv metadata "Collatz + Lee-Yang/partition zeros/large deviations/exponential sums" = 0 hits; Hohertz (https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.08006) is a different object; Bell–Lagarias (https://arxiv.org/abs/1408.6884) is boundaries, not weighted-ensemble zeros; Santana (https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03297) has no computations. The unique rigorous adjacent estimate: Tao's superpolynomial Fourier decay of Syracuse variables at 3-power moduli (https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.03562) — never at the cycle modulus. Benchmarks to beat/complement: Halbeisen–Hungerbühler/Eliahou (http://dml.mathdoc.fr/item/bwmeta1.element.bwnjournal-article-aav78i3p227bwm/), Hercher m ≤ 91; template: Odlyzko–Poonen (http://www.cecm.sfu.ca/organics/papers/odlyzko/paper/html/paper.html).

**The push.** Since #admissible words ≈ 2^{0.95K} while M ≈ 2^K, **any uniform power saving |S_{K,L}(ξ)| ≤ C(K−1,L−1)^{1−δ} for ξ≠0 proves finiteness of Collatz cycles outright** — an equidistribution attack orthogonal to Baker (Baker bounds |M| from below; this bounds *solution counts* in (Z/M)^×, where 2^K ≡ 3^L mod M makes ⟨2,3⟩ nearly cyclic — unexploited structure). Calibrated honestly: full uniformity likely embeds ×2×3-rigidity hardness **[HARD OPEN]**; the realistic deliverable is major-arc decay + ⟨2⟩-orbit symmetry + the *reduction theorem* ("square-root cancellation at cycle moduli ⇒ finitely many cycles"), which exists nowhere. Explicit failure warning from the brief: literal Lee–Yang positivity (circle theorems, Asano contraction) probably does **not** apply — weights are characters, not ferromagnetic couplings; the operative tools are BKW + exponential sums.

**First move.** (i) **[LEMMA, easy]** Symmetry lemma: cyclic word rotation ⇒ |S| constant on ⟨2⟩-orbits in Z/M, collapsing frequency space by ord_M(2); Lean warm-up. (ii) 32-core: transfer-matrix map of |S(ξ)|/C(K−1,L−1) for all admissible signatures K ≤ 5000 near CF convergents of log₂3. (iii) **[PROGRAM]** Transplant Tao's §5–6 skew-walk mechanism from modulus 3^n to modulus M: |S(ξ)| ≤ C·ρ^{t(ξ)} with t counting DP steps where the phase increment is far from 0.

**Success =** the reduction theorem published + empirical square-root cancellation atlas (30–50% likely case), possibly unconditional exclusion of specific (K,L) families beyond Baker. Jackpot (<2–3%): a proven uniform bound ⇒ cycle finiteness — which would exceed everything since Steiner 1977.

### Crack 4 — Exact spectrum of the multiplication-carries chain

**The bridge, precisely.** One Syracuse step is base-2 long multiplication by 3 plus increment; under Haar on Z₂ the pending carry is *exactly* Diaconis–Fulman's carries-for-multiplication chain K_{m,2} (§5.2 of https://arxiv.org/abs/0806.3583), whose spectrum DF explicitly left open ("depends on the number theoretic relation of k and b in a complicated way"). k steps with a odd steps factor through K_{3^a,2}. The briefing agent computed the answer and verified it for m ∈ {3,5,7,9,11,15,27,81}: **det(xI − K_{m,2}) = (x−1)·∏_O (x^{|O|} − 2^{−|O|})** over ⟨2⟩-orbits O on Z/m∖{0} — every subdominant eigenvalue has modulus exactly 1/2, phases 2πj/ord₂(m), and for m = 3^a the spectrum encodes the tower ord₂(3^i) = 2·3^{i−1}, i.e. *the same ⟨2⟩ mod 3^a arithmetic as Hercher's cycle bounds and Tao's characteristic-function decay*, never phrased as carries. Per-step carry count κ = ν₂(C(3n+1,n)) ties Hamming-weight evolution to the chain (Kummer/Legendre).

**Prior-art map (honest).** Addition-carries theory is complete (Holte 1997; DF https://arxiv.org/abs/0806.3583, https://arxiv.org/abs/1102.5159 — Foulkes characters/Eulerian idempotents); exactly 4 papers full-text-match "amazing matrix carries," none mention Collatz; Lagarias's ~350-entry bibliographies contain one carry remark (Cloney–Goles–Vichniac base-6). Multiplication carries: Izsak–Pippenger (https://arxiv.org/abs/0801.4040, single multiplication only). Collatz-side carry work is automata-flavored only: Stérin (https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.00775), Kari (https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-642-31653-1_5), Kurgansky–Potapov (https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.02254), AFS base-3/2 (https://perso.telecom-paristech.fr/jsaka/PUB/Files/RBNS-rev.pdf); fringe 2026 item https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11066. Tao 2019 grep: "carry" never appears.

**The push.** Exact correlation decay 2^{−r} per bit of separation, with computable oscillatory phases, for *every* finite digit-window functional of Haar-random orbits — strictly more than Terras equidistribution (parity marginal only); a rigorous finite-window surrogate process with O(2^{−r}) error bars; and a quantitative obstruction mechanism against small regular divergence certificates (a DFA computing window functionals must distinguish states at correlation scale 2^{−r} ⇒ state-count lower bounds). Honest limits stated plainly: all Haar-measure statements; integers are Haar-null; individual orbits (and Antihydra) invisible; Stewart's s₂(3^n) ≫ log n/log log n wall untouched.

**First move.** *Lemma A* (one week, half Lean): prove the determinant formula — conjugate by characters; the chain c′ ≡ 2^{−1}(c − d′) mod m block-triangularizes into ⟨2⟩-orbit blocks of a g-circulant, each a cyclic weighted shift with determinant 2^{−|O|}. Essentially forced **[LEMMA, ~certain]**. Then 32-core: compose/minimize k-fold Syracuse letter-transducers (k ≤ 8, all parity vectors), test the persistence conjecture (spectrum ⊂ {2^{−r}ζ : ζ^{ord₂(3^a)} = 1}), extract exact joint laws of (parity window, κ-sequence).

**Success =** the DF-open-problem theorem + exact digit-correlation decay for 3x+1 (likely, ~75%); best case (~20%) persistence holds and yields an asymptotic regular-certificate lower bound extending our ≤7-state result. Direct conjecture progress: <1%, by design.

### Crack 5 — Constrained joint spectral radius / path-complete convergence certificates

**The bridge, precisely.** The accelerated Collatz map on the homogeneous lift x = (n,1) is literally a state-feedback switched affine system with A_even = [[1/2,0],[0,1]], A_odd = [[3/2,1/2],[0,1]], switching determined by parity feedback. Divergence = unbounded trajectory of the feedback-constrained system; the constrained JSR (automaton-restricted switching; Philippe et al., arXiv:1503.06984) is "growth under ω-regular switching constraints," and path-complete Lyapunov functions (AJPR, arXiv:1111.3427) are automaton-indexed norm families certifying stability — i.e. **regular convergence certificates, the exact dual of our regular divergence-certificate no-go**. YAH's matrix interpretations are the linear-ranking special case. Known obstruction the bridge must respect (this is the brief's key finding): at every modulus 2^k the parity automaton is full and the 2-adic cycles through −1, −5, −17 embed, forcing the naive constrained JSR to 3/2 at every finite level — so any certificate **must** use the affine +1 term and positivity (cone-constrained JSR, Protasov). That is where the new mathematics lives.

**Prior-art map (honest).** The control community saw the bridge and left it: Blondel–Tsitsiklis undecidability + surveys cite 3x+1 as motivation, prove nothing about it (https://www.mit.edu/~jnt/Papers/J081-00-vb-products.pdf). Zero hits for "Collatz path-complete" / "Collatz constrained JSR." Fringe: sliding-mode analogy (https://www.researchgate.net/publication/354556147_Analogy_Between_the_Collatz_Conjecture_and_Sliding_Mode_Control), Leventides–Poulios Koopman (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2405896321005371). Baseline to beat: YAH (https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.14697). Cautions: Bousch–Mairesse falsity of the finiteness conjecture (optimal growth need not be periodic).

**The push.** Cone-constrained multinorm/SOS certificates on the mod-2^k de Bruijn lift, with the affine term distinguishing N from the negative 2-adic cycles. Best case: a finite certificate excluding positive divergence — would split the conjecture (only finitely-checkable cycles remain, complementing Hercher) **[PROGRAM, 2–5%]**. Structurally-likely case (~60%): a proven no-go "no degree-d SOS multinorm certificate on the mod-2^k lift for all (d,k) ≤ (D,K)" — extending our 7-state Boolean theorem to *analytic/weighted* certificates and mapping where the certificate wall sits. Either outcome is program progress; that asymmetry is why this ranks above prettier cracks.

**First move.** Prove first the **[LEMMA]** that pins the geometry: any homogeneous (C=0, cone-free) certificate is impossible at every finite k because the −1/−5/−17 cycles embed in G_k with growth 3^m/2^n > 1 — this makes every solver failure interpretable. Then the compute sweep: SwitchOnSafety.jl/Mosek SOS feasibility over the mod-2^k transition graph, {V_r} of degree ≤ d positive on the cone x ≥ N₀, V_{T(r)}(T_branch(x)) ≤ λV_r(x) + C, starting (k,d) = (8,2).

**Success =** either the landmark positive-divergence exclusion (unlikely) or the (d,k)-no-go map published in a control/dynamics venue — the first analytic-certificate analogue of the DFA exhaustion.

- **Shadowing (#16).** T|Z₂ ≅ 2-shift has POTP trivially; "which points shadow" restates the integer/2-adic mismatch that *is* the problem. Window-transfer lemmas vs the 2^71 frontier are safe but incremental; fold into general infrastructure, no dedicated effort.
- **Games (#17).** Fraenkel's Mark-game SG theory (https://www.wisdom.weizmann.ac.il/~fraenkel/Papers/DISC8773.pdf) shows division-move games can have automatic SG functions, but nothing transports to ×3+1 moves; sole prior art is a 2010 blog post. SG-compute + DFA-learn the P-set only if cores idle; the hydra-game paper (https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.09719) is math.GM and flagged unreliable.
